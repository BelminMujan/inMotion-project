History

Work Cat Trans Gulf (WCTG) is a short sea shipping operation that provides trans-Gulf of Mexico container and roll on/roll off freight transportation services between the ports of Tampa, Florida and Brownsville, Texas utilizing its own game-changing proprietary vessel design known as the “Work Cat.”
The Company is the exclusive owner and operator of the fast and highly efficient Work Cat cargo vessel—a modern 400 foot catamaran capable of transporting 300 truckload equivalent shipments.
Through its relationships with third party logistics providers, manufactured product shippers, and intermodal carriers, WCTG offers a completely unique alternative form of transportation capable of delivering fast, highly reliable service at low cost relative to all other land and sea-based transport modes.
The economies of scale achieved by the Company’s unique catamaran cargo vessel enables the generation of a growing revenue stream and well above average return on investment.

Management

Work Cat Trans Gulf has an experienced management team that is skilled in maritime cargo marketing, sales, and operations as well as vessel design and construction. The team’s years of experience and diverse backgrounds include national-level executive leadership and new business development.

Hank Hoffman

Chief Executive Officer

Mr. Hoffman has over twenty-five years of executive management experience in the transportation industry. Prior to his role as a founder of Phoenix Trans Gulf, he served as President and Chief Operating Officer of Integrated Freight Corporation, a publicly traded truckload carrier as well as founder and President of SeaBridge Freight, a short sea intermodal barge business. Before joining SeaBridge, he was founder and Chairman of SiriCOMM, Inc., a wireless networking and applications service provider dedicated to the commercial trucking industry. Mr. Hoffman’s trucking experience includes owner and president of Hook Up, Inc., the second largest new truck driveaway service in the U.S. and president of Tri-State Motor Transit, the industry’s largest transporter of munitions, radioactive materials, and hazardous waste. He served as an officer in the United States Army for six years where he completed two successful command assignments. He earned a Bachelor of Science degree from the United States Military Academy, West Point, NY and a MBA from the University of Wisconsin, Oshkosh, WI.
